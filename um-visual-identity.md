# Maastricht University Visual Identity Guidelines

> Last updated: June 2025  
> Source: UM Brandbook / Communicatiegids / House Style Office

---

## 1. Logo

### Primary Logo
The UM logo features **two vertically aligned, slightly offset blue triangles** enclosing the letters 'U' and 'M'. This iconic design was unveiled in **January 1999** and will be retained in the current visual identity refresh.

### Logo Variations
- **Standard**: Blue triangles on white/light backgrounds
- **Reversed**: White triangles on dark backgrounds
- **UM-MUMC+ Collaboration**: Logo order depends on dominant party in the collaboration

### Logo Usage Rules
- Never distort, rotate, or modify the logo proportions
- Maintain clear space around the logo (minimum = height of the "U")
- Never place the logo on busy backgrounds without sufficient contrast

The visual identity is being refreshed to include an **expanded color palette** with:
- Browns

- Greens

### Background Elements: "Gestapelde Stenen"
The **stacked stone shapes** (gestapelde stenen) are used as background elements:
- Lightened using brandbook "lichte kleuren" at **0.4 opacity**
- Creates subtle texture without overwhelming content

## 3. Typography

### Primary Font: **Fort**
Fort is the custom UM font used for both headlines and body text.

### Fallback Font: **Aptos**
When Fort is unavailable (especially in Microsoft Office applications like Word and PowerPoint), use **Aptos** as the system font.

**Why Aptos:**
- High legibility in text-heavy documents
- Clean, contemporary appearance
- Slight warmth in design
- Native to Windows/Microsoft 365

### Typography Usage Hierarchy
1. **Headlines**: Fort Bold (or Aptos Bold)
2. **Subheadings**: Fort SemiBold
3. **Body Text**: Fort Regular (or Aptos Regular)
4. **Captions/Footnotes**: Fort Light or Aptos at smaller sizes

### Font Sizing Guidelines
- **H1 Headlines**: 32-48pt
- **H2 Subheadings**: 24-28pt
- **Body Text**: 11-14pt
- **Captions**: 9-10pt

## 4. Design Principles

### Border Radius
- **Cards/UI elements**: `32px` border-radius
- Buttons and containers follow this rounded corner aesthetic

### Spacing & Layout
- Maintain generous whitespace
- Use the stone shapes subtly as background texture
- Keep content aligned to a clear grid system

### Photography Style
- Authentic, candid imagery preferred
- Show real students, staff, and campus life
- Avoid generic stock photography
- Natural lighting over studio setups

## 5. Transparency & Disclosure

### When to Disclose AI Involvement
| Content Type | Disclosure Required? | Placement |
|--------------|----------------------|-----------|
| Press Release (AI-drafted) | Yes | Footer note |
| Social Media Post (AI-drafted) | Optional | Alt text or comments |
| Translation (AI-assisted) | No | Internal log only |
| Image (AI-generated) | Yes | Image caption |
| Crisis Communication | N/A | No AI used |
| Executive Statement | N/A | No AI used |

### Standard Disclosure Language
- "Drafted with AI assistance, reviewed and approved by [Name]"
- "AI-generated image"
- "Translation: AI-assisted with human review"
